\# Space Planning Requirements



\## General Planning

\### (Section 1)

A. All building layouts shall prioritize \*\*functional efficiency\*\*, \*\*maintenance access\*\*, and \*\*flexibility for future use\*\*.  

B. Coordinate with the UCA Physical Plant Department early in the design process to confirm functional adjacencies and departmental space needs.  

C. Layouts shall provide adequate space for mechanical and electrical equipment rooms, janitorial closets, and storage areas.



---



\## Accessibility and Circulation

\### (Section 2)

A. Provide barrier-free access throughout all facilities in accordance with the \*\*Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)\*\* and \*\*Arkansas Accessibility Code\*\*.  

B. Corridors shall be clear, direct, and allow easy navigation between departments.  

C. Include accessible restrooms on each floor.  

D. Avoid dead-end corridors longer than 20 feet.



---



\## Public Spaces

\### (Section 3)

A. Main building entries shall be \*\*visible and clearly defined\*\* by architectural form, canopy, or signage.  

B. Design lobbies and reception areas to handle visitor traffic comfortably and securely.  

C. Where applicable, include space for \*\*public information displays\*\*, \*\*digital directories\*\*, or \*\*student postings\*\*.  

D. Locate water fountains, vending, and public restrooms near major circulation routes.



---



\## Departmental Spaces

\### (Section 4)

A. Department layouts shall promote \*\*logical flow\*\*, with administrative, instructional, and support functions appropriately grouped.  

B. Offices and conference rooms should have \*\*visual privacy\*\* but maintain transparency for safety when appropriate.  

C. Provide adequate \*\*file and supply storage\*\* within each department.  

D. Furniture layouts should be verified with UCA before construction documents are finalized.



---



\## Mechanical, Electrical, and Data Coordination

\### (Section 5)

A. Provide adequate service clearances for \*\*mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and technology systems\*\*.  

B. Ensure that all \*\*electrical and data rooms\*\* are fully accessible and sized per equipment manufacturer requirements.  

C. Coordinate \*\*roof equipment placement\*\* with UCA’s Physical Plant to maintain serviceability and visual consistency across campus.  

D. Provide \*\*access panels\*\* or removable ceiling tiles for all concealed equipment.



---



\## Building Support Spaces

\### (Section 6)

A. Provide \*\*custodial closets\*\* on each floor, sized for cart and supply storage.  

B. Provide \*\*mechanical rooms\*\* large enough for safe equipment maintenance and replacement.  

C. \*\*Electrical rooms\*\* shall have minimum clearances per NEC and house power distribution, telecom, and fire alarm equipment.  

D. \*\*IT closets and data rooms\*\* shall include dedicated cooling, grounding, and secure access.



---



\## Restrooms

\### (Section 7)

A. Locate \*\*multi-stall restrooms\*\* near major circulation corridors.  

B. Provide \*\*family/uni-sex restrooms\*\* in larger facilities, near public areas or accessible routes.  

C. Coordinate fixture counts and layouts with Arkansas Plumbing Code and ADA standards.  

D. \*\*Private offices and suites\*\* may include single-user restrooms only when approved by UCA.



---



\## Break Areas and Lounges

\### (Section 8)

A. Provide break areas conveniently located for staff, but not within primary public view.  

B. Include \*\*counter space\*\*, \*\*sink\*\*, \*\*microwave\*\*, \*\*refrigerator\*\*, and \*\*trash/recycling\*\* provisions.  

C. Coordinate plumbing and electrical rough-ins with mechanical and electrical design teams.  

D. Include durable, easy-to-clean finishes in all break areas.



---



\## Circulation and Storage

\### (Section 9)

A. Provide clear, code-compliant travel paths throughout the building.  

B. Avoid unnecessary changes in direction and maintain consistent corridor widths.  

C. Provide \*\*general storage rooms\*\* at each floor level, accessible to staff.  

D. Storage areas shall include adjustable shelving and adequate lighting.



---



\## Special Rooms and Functions

\### (Section 10)

A. \*\*Lactation Rooms:\*\*  

&nbsp;  - Provide in larger buildings, excluding dormitories and Greek houses.  

&nbsp;  - Include sink, counter, changing station, comfortable chair, side table, and outlets nearby.  

&nbsp;  - No refrigerator.  

B. \*\*Prayer / Reflection Rooms:\*\*  

&nbsp;  - If requested, provide small private space with washable floor finishes.  

C. \*\*Volunteer Sign-In and Community Spaces:\*\*  

&nbsp;  - Provide flexible areas adjacent to lobbies or public entrances where needed.  

D. \*\*Care Coordination and Interview Spaces:\*\*  

&nbsp;  - Provide small, private meeting rooms with sound isolation and visibility for safety.



---



\## Furniture and Equipment

\### (Section 11)

A. Coordinate furniture layouts with UCA prior to construction documentation.  

B. Maintain flexibility for modular systems and future technology upgrades.  

C. Built-in furniture should use durable, low-maintenance materials consistent with UCA finish standards.  

D. All casework and counters in public areas shall use \*\*solid surfacing\*\* at tops and \*\*high-pressure laminate\*\* at vertical surfaces unless noted otherwise.



---



\## Final Coordination

\### (Section 12)

A. Confirm all room uses and spatial adjacencies with UCA during schematic design.  

B. Review space lists and furniture layouts during design development.  

C. Provide updated plans for Physical Plant review prior to construction documents.  

D. Ensure consistency with the \*\*UCA General Building Requirements\*\* document and all related divisions.



---



\## Summary

\### (Section 13)

This section establishes planning and layout standards to ensure that all new and renovated spaces on the University of Central Arkansas campus are functional, maintainable, and aligned with university-wide design and accessibility goals.
