\# Architect and Engineer Instructions



\## Submit Plans to the Conway Fire Department

\### (Section 1)

A. The state fire marshal does have review authority; however, he relies on the local jurisdiction for review.



---



\## Conway Corporation Coordination

\### (Section 2)

A. Complete Conway Corporation Utility Service Requirement Form.  

B. Meet with Conway Corp as needed to coordinate project utility requirements and future CC plans.



---



\## Meeting Minutes

\### (Section 3)

A. The Lead Consultant shall be responsible for taking notes at all meetings between their office and University personnel, including telephone conversations, for all phases of services covered under the Contract Agreement.  

B. Submit meeting minutes to the Owner’s Representative no later than the third working day following the meeting or conversation.  

C. The Owner’s Representative will distribute copies to the appropriate parties.



---



\## Room Numbering

\### (Section 4)

A. Provide to the Owner a floor plan devoid of room numbers so the Owner may number the rooms.  

B. If the Consultant is directed by UCA to number the rooms:

&nbsp;  1. Use four (4) digit numbers for stair towers, corridors, and elevator lobbies.  

&nbsp;  2. Number rooms sequentially starting in the northwest corner and move clockwise around the building.



---



\## Submit Digital Drawings to Physical Plant

\### (Section 5)

A. Submit a complete set of construction drawings in digital format to the Physical Plant at the same time paper copies of the bid set are delivered.  

B. Contact the UCA Physical Plant Department for \*\*preferred media\*\* and \*\*CAD software version\*\* prior to submission.



---



\## Site Design

\### (Section 6)

A. Ensure positive site drainage to prevent water from flooding buildings.  

B. Implement proper grading and drainage systems to direct water away from structures.  

C. For buildings set away from paving with exterior MEP access, provide a 6-foot walk to the mechanical or electrical rooms.  

&nbsp;  - Walks should include soft radii for golf cart access, as UCA uses golf carts (“buggies”) frequently.



---



\## Lactation Rooms

\### (Section 7)

A. In larger buildings (not including dormitories and Greek houses), include a \*\*Lactation Room\*\*.  

B. Provide the following:

&nbsp;  - Sink, counter, and changing station.  

&nbsp;  - Space for a comfortable chair and side table with nearby electrical outlets.  

&nbsp;  - \*Do not include a refrigerator.\*



---



\## Uni-Sex Restrooms

\### (Section 8)

A. In larger buildings, provide a convenient \*\*Uni-sex / Family restroom.\*\*  

B. Include:

&nbsp;  - A fold-up changing table.  

&nbsp;  - Ample floor and counter space, if possible.



---



\## Checklist

\### (Section 9)

A. Submit plans to the \*\*Conway Fire Department\*\* — while the state fire marshal retains review authority, local jurisdiction conducts reviews.  

B. Complete \*\*Conway Corporation Utility Service Requirement Form.\*\*  

C. Meet with Conway Corp as needed to coordinate project utility requirements and future CC plans.  

D. Provide to the Owner a floor plan without room numbers so that numbering can be assigned by UCA.  

E. Submit all construction drawings digitally to the Physical Plant in accordance with current CAD standards.  

F. Coordinate with the UCA Physical Plant Department for CAD software version and delivery media preferences.



---



\## Additional Coordination Requirements

\### (Section 10)

A. Coordinate with all relevant UCA departments prior to finalizing plans, including:

&nbsp;  - Physical Plant  

&nbsp;  - Conway Fire Department  

&nbsp;  - Conway Corporation Utility  

&nbsp;  - UCA Construction Management  

B. Verify that all drawing submissions include the latest updates and comply with the University’s current standards.



---



\## Summary

\### (Section 11)

This section establishes the procedural and administrative standards required of Architects and Engineers working with the University of Central Arkansas.  

It defines responsibilities for coordination, documentation, and digital submissions that ensure projects meet institutional expectations.
