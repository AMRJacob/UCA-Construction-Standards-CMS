## General Conditions

### (Section 1)

1. Utilize AIA Document A201 – 2007 Edition – General Conditions for the Contract of Construction

## Weather Delays

### (Section 2)

1. Per **AIA A201. 4.3.7.2 Weather Delays**
   1. Include in construction time stated in Contract documents an allowance for calendar days per month which, according to the following inclement weather table may not be available for construction out-of-doors (normal inclement weather).
   2. Contract time will not be extended due to normal inclement weather unless the Contractor can substantiate, to satisfaction of Architect, that greater-than-normal inclement weather occurred, considering the full term of contract Time, using 5-year average of accumulated record mean values from climatological data compiled by the U.S. Department of commerce National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration for the project local, and that alleged greater-then-normal inclement weather actually delayed work or portions of work.
   3. All inclement weather day extension submittals must be submitted to the Architect for review and approval by the 15th of the following month.
   4. The measure of extreme weather shall be the number of days in excess of those stated for each month, in precipitation exceeded 0.10 inch, from area weather station for same period of time, which is source of data used to determine normal weather losses.
   5. If total accumulated number of calendar days lost to weather exceeds total accumulated number expected for same period from inclement weather table, time for completion will be extended by number of calendar days needed to include excess number of calendar days lost.
      - January (7)
      - February (5)
      - March (7)
      - April (7)
      - May (8)
      - June (6)
      - July (6)
      - August (4)
      - September (6)
      - October (3)
      - November (6)
      - December (4)

## Requests for information (RFI)

### (Section 3)

1. To be submitted using AIA Document G716 – 2004 Edition

## Architects Electronic File Release

### (Section 4)

1. AIA specific forms are not required for this purpose.
2. UCA is considered to -own- the documents, and as a matter of policy will not pay Architect for electronic copies of drawings.
3. UCA may sign a release of liability form, to obtain the electronic files.
4. Consult with UCA project manager whether Contractor may pay for the electronic files.
5. Subject to payment & liability release agreements, Architect shall share electronic files with Contractor for clash detection, preparation of shop drawings and other purposes.
6. Architect shall provide files in their native forms, 2D or 3D drawings and models, as image files (.pdfs), and/or translated into necessary file formats, within the Architect's office ability.
