# Procurement & Contracting

## Weather Delays

**Reference:** AIA Document A201 \-- 2007 Edition \-- General Conditions for the Contract of Construction \- AIA A201. 4.3.7.2

Include in construction time stated in Contract documents an allowance for calendar days per month which, according to the following inclement weather table may not be available for construction out-of-doors (normal inclement weather).

### Monthly Weather Day Allowances

| Month | Allowance (Days) |
| :---- | :---- |
| January | 7 |
| February | 5 |
| March | 7 |
| April | 7 |
| May | 8 |
| June | 6 |
| July | 6 |
| August | 4 |
| September | 6 |
| October | 3 |
| November | 6 |
| December | 4 |

### Weather Delay Extension Requirements

- Contract time will not be extended due to normal inclement weather unless the Contractor can substantiate, to satisfaction of Architect, that greater-than-normal inclement weather occurred  
- Must consider the full term of contract Time, using 5-year average of accumulated record mean values from climatological data compiled by the U.S. Department of Commerce National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration for the project local  
- Must prove that alleged greater-than-normal inclement weather actually delayed work or portions of work

### Submittal Requirements

- **All inclement weather day extension submittals must be submitted to the Architect for review and approval by the 15th of the following month**

### Measurement of Extreme Weather

- The measure of extreme weather shall be the number of days in excess of those stated for each month, in precipitation exceeded 0.10 inch, from area weather station for same period of time  
- This weather station is the source of data used to determine normal weather losses

### Extension Calculation

If total accumulated number of calendar days lost to weather exceeds total accumulated number expected for same period from inclement weather table, time for completion will be extended by number of calendar days needed to include excess number of calendar days lost.

## Architects Electronic File Release

**Reference:** Section 006520

### File Ownership and Release Policy

- AIA specific forms are not required for this purpose  
- **UCA is considered to "own" the documents**, and as a matter of policy will not pay Architect for electronic copies of drawings  
- UCA may sign a release of liability form to obtain the electronic files  
- Consult with UCA project manager whether Contractor may pay for the electronic files

### File Sharing Requirements

Subject to payment & liability release agreements, Architect shall share electronic files with Contractor for:

- Clash detection  
- Preparation of shop drawings  
- Other purposes

### File Format Requirements

Architect shall provide files in:

- Their native forms (2D or 3D drawings and models)  
- Image files (.pdfs)  
- Translated into necessary file formats, within the Architect's office ability
