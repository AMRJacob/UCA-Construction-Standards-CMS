### Compliance with Arkansas Sustainable Energy-Efficient Buildings Program

## Definitions

### (Section 1)

1. Construction of major facilities (larger than 20,000 gross square feet) and major renovations (cost is over 50% of current replacement value of building, greater than 20,000 sq. ft. and funded at least in part by the State of Arkansas) at UCA are governed by the requirements of Arkansas Code Title 22, Chapter 2, as amended by Act 1494. Design professionals and the construction contractors involved in such projects have the duty and responsibility to ensure the project complies with Act 1494.

## Reporting

### (Section 2)

1. The design professional shall at the conceptual design phase inform the Director, Physical Plant or his representative of the specific measures, which will be included in the project design to comply with Act 1494. Periodically during the design phase and thereafter throughout construction, the designer and builder shall report on the status of compliance measures. Revisions, where necessary, shall not compromise the integrity of the project insofar as compliance with Act 1494 is concerned.

## Standards

## (Section 3)

1. Specific standards drawn from Act 1494 that pertain are described below:

&nbsp;  1. Energy Usage Reduction. The project shall "be designed, constructed and certified to at least ten percent (10%) reduction below the baseline energy consumption determined in accordance with the Performance Rating Method of Appendix G of the American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air Conditioning Engineers Standard 90.1-2007, as it existed on January 1, 2009."

&nbsp;  2. Life Cycle Cost Analysis. For building components and systems which affect energy consumption, designers shall employ life cycle cost analysis of two or more alternatives, using current United States Department of Energy economic assumptions. The lowest cost alternative may not always be the one used. The Director, Physical Plant will evaluate the alternatives from these economic analyses and make appropriate recommendations.

&nbsp;  3. Indoor Potable Water Use Reduction. For new construction, the indoor water system shall "be designed and constructed to use at least twenty percent (20%) less potable water than the indoor water use baseline calculated for the building after satisfying the fixture performance requirement, if any under the Arkansas Plumbing Code."

&nbsp;  4. Outdoor Potable Water Use Reduction. For new construction, the consumption of outdoor potable water shall be reduced by fifty percent (50%) of the water that would have been consumed with conventional landscape and irrigation strategies. Landscape design and irrigation shall make use of efficient landscape materials and irrigation strategies, including without limitation water reuse and recycling to accomplish the reduction.

&nbsp;  5. Utility Metering. Building level meters shall be provided and installed for electricity, natural gas, and any other energy sources utilized, including chilled water and hot water from other plants. Potable water used indoors and outdoors shall metered separately. Guidelines for meter installation are issued by the US Department of Energy in Section 103 of the Energy Policy Act of 2005.

&nbsp;  6. Commissioning. The major facility and major renovation projects shall employ "building or system commissioning practices that are tailored to the size and complexity of the building and its system components." The Commissioning agent shall be assigned to the project and be involved in the design effort on all projects exceeding five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000) in construction cost. Design involvement by the commissioning agent may be used but is not required for commissioning of smaller projects. In any case, the interim and final commissioning reports shall be delivered the Director, Physical Plant.

&nbsp;  7. Operational Data Collection/Compliance Review. For the first twelve (12) months of building operation following project completion the Director, Engineering Systems shall document metered energy and water usage and provide this information to the project designer. The project designer shall prepare a report comparing metered data with the energy and water use design targets. This report will be presented to the UCA Board of Trustees following review and comment by UCA officials. If the metered utility consumption exceeds the standards of Act 1494, as documented in the report above, the designer, construction contractor, commissioning agent and UCA staff shall investigate, determine the cause of failure and recommend corrections or modifications to meet the standards.
